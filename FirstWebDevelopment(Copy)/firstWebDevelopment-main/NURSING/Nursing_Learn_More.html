Admission and Retention Policies

Incoming Freshman

- High school card should have an average of 80% and above;

- Entrance test score should be 40 and above;

- Level of competency during interview should be 60% and above;

- Incoming student must have undergone thorough physical examination.

Transferees

- Transferees must comply with the abovementioned requirements except for the submission of the high school card.

- In addition, transferees must submit a photocopy of his/her TOR for evaluation purposes.

For a student to be eligible to enroll in BSN II and III, they must:

- Pass all major nursing subjects;
- Have received complete doses of Hepatitis B immunization;
- Pass the Nursing Aptitude Test/Assessment for Nursing Potential (a requisite to NCM 100.)
- Have attended the official Cap and Badge Ceremony which required before a student can go on duty.
- Follow the RLE policies stipulated in the RLE handbook.

For students to be eligible to enroll in BSN IV, they must:

- Incoming BSN II must have undergone thorough physical examination.
-Pass all the major nursing subjects and has taken up all minor subjects in the Levels I, II, III.
- Pass the practical exam in the 1st semester and the oral exam in the second semester.



Board Exam Parameters

To qualify for the June Board Exam the student must obtain the following marks:

Pre-Board Exam Result of at least 40% ii. Cumulative grade from NCM 100 – NCM 121 A of 30% iii. Oral Examination Result – 30% June Board Exam Review

Transferees

All DMCCF College of Nursing graduates except second coursers, who will qualify to take the November licensure examination for the first time are required to take the supervised review before they are allowed to take the said examination

Grading System

The semestral grade is the average grade of all four terms (Prelim, Midterm, Semi and Finals). Each term grade is 25%


- In NCM 100 to NCM 121A, lecture is 60% and RLE is 40% ; RLE grade is based on skills, knowledge, and attitude;


- Student must pay the special exam fee of Fifty Pesos (Php 50.00) at the Accounting Office and must secure an absent slip dully signed by the Dean of the College of Nursing with attached medical certificate for them to be given the special exam.
