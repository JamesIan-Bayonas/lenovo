OUR STORY

DMC College Foundation, located in Dipolog City, Philippines, has a rich history dating back to 1974 when it was founded as a 50-bed general hospital. The college started offering midwifery courses in 1977 and later expanded to offer nursing, physical therapy, radiologic technology, and medical technology programs. In 1994, the college was renamed Dipolog Medical Center College of Health and Sciences and later became a non-profit organization in 1998. Over the years, the college has continued to grow and expand its programs, including the addition of secondary education, computer science, criminology, and business courses. The college has also established partnerships with other institutions, such as LaSallian Schools Supervision Office and Negros Oriental State University, to offer graduate programs and vocational courses. Today, DMC College Foundation offers 21 bachelor's degree programs, 4 associate programs, and 16 vocational courses, and is recognized as a solid and successful institution in the region



FURTHER CONTEXT

DMC College Foundation, located in Dipolog City, Philippines, has a rich history dating back to 1974 when it was founded as a 50-bed general hospital by 15 Dipolog citizens, including then Dipolog Archbishop Msgr. Felix Sanchez Zafra and 15 medical practitioners. The college started offering midwifery courses in 1977 and later expanded to offer nursing, physical therapy, radiologic technology, and medical technology programs. In 1989, a new management team took over, and the college was renamed Dipolog Medical Center College of Health Sciences in 1991. The college continued to grow and expand its programs, including the addition of secondary education, computer science, criminology, and business courses.


MISSION

We set high standards in the performance of our students, faculty and co-academic personnel to achieve optimum integral human development by continuously enriching our academic, co-academic and leadership programs with technology-based instruction, team research and the promotion of athletics, culture and arts grounded on nationalism.
