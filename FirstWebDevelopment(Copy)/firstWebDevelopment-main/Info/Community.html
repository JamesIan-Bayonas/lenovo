COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT

DMC College Foundation, located in Dipolog City, Philippines, has a rich history dating back to 1974 when it was founded as a 50-bed general hospital. The college started offering midwifery courses in 1977 and later expanded to offer nursing, physical therapy, radiologic technology, and medical technology programs. In 1994, the college was renamed Dipolog Medical Center College of Health and Sciences and later became a non-profit organization in 1998. Over the years, the college has continued to grow and expand its programs, including the addition of secondary education, computer science, criminology, and business courses. The college has also established partnerships with other institutions, such as LaSallian Schools Supervision Office and Negros Oriental State University, to offer graduate programs and vocational courses. Today, DMC College Foundation offers 21 bachelor's degree programs, 4 associate programs, and 16 vocational courses, and is recognized as a solid and successful institution in the region



OBJECTIVES

1. To promote extension projects through self-help process in target community. 2. To implement programs and services that target beneficiary could undertake in their own community. 3. Design a program wherein target community will serve as channel for technology development, promotion, adoption and utilization 4. Improve the quality of life of deprived and underprivileged communities by implementing viable projects and programs. 5. Coordinate with other sectors of the school community e.g. all schools/colleges, offices and departments, the students including the alumni and other agencies GOs and NGOs for complementation of services.


MISSION

To provide appropriate services needed by the community and improve their living condition through viable programs and services in cooperation and coordination with different agencies and entities.

VISION

To improve quality of life and have a greater impact on the communities being served with special focus to the underprivileged.
